Trust layer

AgentBond

"Can I trust this agent
before it acts?"

USDC collateral behind an agent's reputation. The agent grants enforcers a slash allowance; counterparties lock a slice of the free bond behind a job, then release on performance or slash on default. Ownerless, balance-delta accounting, the free bond is a public, slashable credit score.

Settlement layer

StreamPay

"Can I pay per second
of work, not per invoice?"

USDC that accrues linearly, by the second. Pay-per-call, per-task or per-second subscriptions. The recipient withdraws what has vested; the sender cancels and reclaims the unvested remainder. No invoices, no chargebacks.

Mechanism layer

CommitStakeV2

"Who pays when
the verifier lies?"

A bonded verifier escrow. A staker commits USDC behind a goal; the verifier attests pass/fail. A two-sided challenge routes to a named arbiter, on an overturn or a no-show the verifier's bonded slice is slashed, the harmed party gets only damage, and the §7a surplus is burned to 0x…dEaD. Writes zero new custody code, it drives the two primitives above.

Counterparty

Business or protocol hiring the agent. Reads the bond to gauge trust, locks collateral against the job, streams payment per second of work delivered, and can stake a goal whose verdict is itself bonded.

← read trust · lock · pay

Arc L1 · USDC = gas

Deterministic finality, dollar-denominated fees. The agent budgets, pays gas, posts bond and gets paid in one unit, no FX, no native-token juggling, no bridges in the trust path.
